🔒 Data Governance & Student Privacy

Your data. Your control. Always.

DegreePath is a planning companion — not an official student information system. All academic information you analyze here is voluntarily submitted and used exclusively to generate graduation planning recommendations.

🙋

Student-Controlled Information

All academic information analyzed by DegreePath is voluntarily submitted by you. We do not retrieve official student records from colleges or universities unless an authorized institutional integration is in place.

📋

Minimum Data Collection

The MVP collects only what's needed to generate recommendations. Required fields are limited to university, catalog year, major, completed courses, and earned credits.

  • No Student ID numbers
  • No Social Security Numbers
  • No Financial Aid or Medical records
  • No Banking or Immigration data
📄

Transcript Processing

If a transcript is uploaded, DegreePath extracts only the academic information required for analysis. The original document may be deleted after processing unless you choose to retain it.

🎯

Planning Estimates Only

Recommendations are based on official published degree requirements, public university catalogs, and your self-reported academic history. Always confirm with your advisor before registering or applying to graduate.

⚖️

Student Ownership

You maintain full control over your submitted information. You may update, delete, or export your academic profile and remove any uploaded documents at any time.

🔐

Data Security

DegreePath applies industry-standard administrative, technical, and physical safeguards. Your academic data is used exclusively for planning recommendations. We do not sell student information.

🏛️
No Institutional Authority. DegreePath does not certify graduation eligibility, approve transfer credit, replace official degree audits, modify institutional records, or issue academic decisions on behalf of any college or university. Final academic decisions remain the responsibility of your educational institution. DegreePath serves as a planning companion — not an official academic record.
📋 MVP Charter v1.0

Governing Principles

Every feature in DegreePath is measured against this charter. If it doesn't help a student graduate faster or make better academic decisions, it doesn't belong in the MVP.

Mission Statement

DegreePath is an AI-powered academic planning platform that helps college students understand their progress toward graduation by analyzing student-provided academic information while protecting their privacy and presenting clear, trustworthy, and actionable recommendations.

The MVP is not intended to replace university degree audits or academic advisors. Its purpose is to simplify academic planning and help students make informed decisions before meeting with their institution.

Core Design Principles

Principle 1

Student First

Every feature must answer one question: Does this help a student graduate faster or make better academic decisions? If the answer is "no," it does not belong in the MVP.

Principle 2

Privacy by Design

DegreePath collects only the information necessary to perform an academic analysis. The platform does not function as a student records repository — it creates a temporary Academic Profile based on student-provided information.

Principle 3

Explainable AI

Every recommendation must include an understandable explanation. Students should never wonder why the system made a recommendation.

"Calculus II is recommended because it unlocks four required courses and is offered only in the Fall."
Principle 4

Simplicity

The application should be understandable in less than one minute by a first-time user. No technical language. No catalog terminology unless requested.

Principle 5

Trust

Every recommendation must originate from official university catalogs, Florida degree requirements, published prerequisite rules, the Florida SCNS, and student-provided academic information. The AI never invents academic requirements.

🔒 Privacy-First Processing Workflow

1

Student Retrieves Academic Progress

The student signs into their university portal and downloads their Curriculum Progress, Degree Audit, or Academic Progress Report. Accepted: PDF, Screenshot (PNG/JPG), or degree audit export. No institutional login credentials are shared with DegreePath.

2

Upload

The student uploads the document to DegreePath. The document is held only for processing.

3

OCR & Document Recognition

The AI identifies university, degree program, catalog year, completed courses, credit hours, GPA (optional), and degree requirements shown in the report.

AI Processing
4

Privacy Scan

Before analysis begins, DegreePath automatically detects and masks: student name, Student ID, email, phone, postal address, date of birth, QR codes, barcodes, and any other recognized personal identifiers.

Auto-Masking
5

Student Privacy Review

The student is shown a sanitized preview — masked personal info alongside visible academic data. The student must confirm personal information has been removed and academic information is accurate. Analysis begins only after confirmation.

Student Confirms
6

Academic Profile Creation

DegreePath creates a temporary Academic Profile containing only: institution, catalog year, intended major, completed courses, earned credits, and optional GPA. The original uploaded document is deleted automatically unless the student explicitly chooses to retain it.

Minimal Data
7

AI Analysis

The AI compares the Academic Profile against official program requirements and generates: degree completion %, remaining requirements, recommended next courses, prerequisite analysis, graduation timeline estimate, and academic alerts.

AI Analysis
8

Results Dashboard

Student receives: Academic Health Score, Degree Progress %, Remaining Credits, Recommended Next Courses (prioritized by prerequisite dependencies), Graduation Estimate, Academic Alerts, and plain-language AI Explanations.
